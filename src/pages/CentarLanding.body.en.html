The problem

Projects don't fail because people are bad.
They fail because nothing was written down.

No renovation ever goes exactly to plan. There are always a dozen changes that aren't in the contract: different tiles, two extra outlets, a wall moved half a metre. They get agreed verbally — in the dust, between two phone calls, while both sides are in a hurry.

Months later, both sides remember honestly. And differently.

The contractor loses

Payment for work actually done. Weeks spent proving it. Peaceful nights before handover. Sometimes a client who would have referred them on.

The investor loses

Control over a figure that keeps rising without warning. The chance to say "no" before the work is done. Trust in the person they let into their home.

Same cause. Two casualties. Nobody's ill intent.

The solution · Decisions

Every change goes through the same process. For both of you.

Instead of a verbal handshake — a short, clear process that takes a minute.

Proposal

The contractor describes the change, enters a price, attaches a photo from the site.

Response

The investor accepts, declines, or counters. The contractor can revise once. The negotiation is written down, not retold.

Confirmation

If the decision carries a price, the contract value rises or falls by exactly that amount the moment it's accepted. A change can also make the job cheaper — if something is dropped, the contract goes down with it.

New phase

If the change is extra work with a positive price, Centar automatically creates a new project phase from it — with the exact amount owed by the investor written in.

Deadline

The investor gets 24 hours and an automatic reminder. Without their confirmation, there is no change to the contract and no new phase — an expired deadline is not consent. The reminder is there so nothing is forgotten, not to apply pressure.

Record

At the end: a PDF with the full sequence — who, when, what, and for how much. Sent to both parties by email.

And if you later agree differently — reversal is possible, but only with both sides confirming. The money goes back, the trace stays. Neither side can erase what happened on their own.

The story

Centar wasn't born in a pitch deck.
It was born on a building site.

Behind Centar stands a contractor with thirteen years in renovations and refits. Turn-key work, from demolition to handover.

I built it because I needed it. For years I ran projects with notebooks and messages, and paid for changes out of my own pocket — not because anyone was dishonest, but because nobody had anything in hand. Memory against memory.

I saw the other side too: an investor who ends up with a bigger bill than expected and no way to check how it got there. Their anger is as legitimate as mine.

That's why Centar wasn't built for anyone to win. It was built so nobody has to argue.

— contractor, 13 years on site

Frequently asked

The questions everyone asks.

Does the investor need to have the app?

Yes — you invite them, they open an account for free, and they pay nothing. Without both sides there's no confirmation, and without confirmation there's no record. That's exactly why Decisions work.

Why would an investor agree to this at all?

Because it's just as much in their favour: they see every price before the work happens, nothing moves without their confirmation, and at the end they have a list of everything they agreed to. For most investors, this is the first time they've had any real control over changes.

Does a Decision PDF hold up legally?

It isn't a contract and doesn't replace one. But it is a clean, time-stamped record that both sides confirmed in the app — incomparably more than a message or a memory.

What if the investor doesn't respond within 24 hours?

Nothing changes. The decision stays pending, both sides get a reminder and a note that the deadline has passed. Silence isn't consent — without confirmation there is no change to the contract and no new phase.

Can anyone later change or delete a decision?

Not alone. Reversal requires both sides to confirm, and even then the trace stays on record. The rule applies equally to the contractor and the investor.

Does it work on iPhone?

For now, Android. The app also runs in the browser, on any device.

What about my data?

It's yours. Export it whenever you want, delete your account whenever you want.

Can I use only Projekti, without the rest?

Yes. Every module stands on its own.
